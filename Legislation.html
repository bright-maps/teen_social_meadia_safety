Legislation

In today's digital age, it's crucial to understand the legal landscape surrounding social media use and to implement best practices to ensure a positive and secure online experience for teenagers.

Laws on Social Media

General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)

The GDPR is a regulation in EU law on data protection and privacy in the European Union (EU) and the European Economic Area (EEA). It aims primarily to give control to individuals over their personal data.

Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA)

COPPA is a U.S. law designed to protect the online privacy of children under 13 by requiring parental consent for the collection or use of any personal information of young children.

Electronic Communications Privacy Act (ECPA)

ECPA is a U.S. federal statute that extends government restrictions on wiretaps from telephone calls to electronic data transmissions. It prohibits the unauthorized interception or access of electronic communications.

Guidance and Best Practices

Here are some guidance and best practices to help parents navigate social media usage:

  • Establish clear rules and boundaries for social media use
  • Encourage open communication with teenagers about their online activities
  • Teach teenagers about online privacy and security
